There are three realistic ways to track habits: paper, a dedicated app, or a spreadsheet. Each has real trade-offs. Here's an honest comparison.

## Paper (bullet journal, habit grid, printed template)

**Pros:**
- Zero friction to start — pen and paper is always available
- Physical act of marking a checkbox creates a stronger feedback signal than tapping a screen
- No notifications, no app updates, no accounts
- Visible at a glance if kept on your desk

**Cons:**
- No reminders
- Easy to lose or forget
- No historical data analysis
- Doesn't travel well

**Best for:** People who already use a planner or journal. If your notebook is already open every morning, adding a habit grid takes 30 seconds.

---

## Dedicated app

**Pros:**
- Reminders and notifications
- Streak tracking built in
- Available on your phone, which is always with you
- Some apps offer insights and statistics

**Cons:**
- Most habit apps are over-engineered — they add friction through complexity
- Subscription costs on many popular options
- Easy to ignore notifications after a few days
- Data is locked in the app

**Best for:** People who live on their phone and respond well to reminders. Works best if you keep the app on your home screen and check it first thing.

---

## Spreadsheet (Google Sheets, Excel, Notion)

**Pros:**
- Fully customisable
- Easy to analyse data — streaks, completion rates, trends
- Free
- Data is yours and portable

**Cons:**
- Requires setup time
- Not convenient on mobile
- No reminders unless you build them
- Easy to over-engineer and abandon

**Best for:** People who are already in spreadsheets daily, or who want to analyse their habit data over time.

---

## What actually matters

The method matters less than the consistency of use. A paper grid you check every morning beats an app you open twice a week.

The best tracking system is the one with the least friction for your specific situation. If you're always at a desk, a printed grid works. If you're always on your phone, an app works. If you like data, a spreadsheet works.

Pick one. Use it for 30 days before switching. Most people who "try everything" are avoiding the actual work of building the habit.

---

## One rule regardless of method

Track daily, not weekly. Weekly reviews are useful for reflection, but the tracking itself needs to happen on the day. Memory is unreliable — trying to fill in a week's worth of checkboxes on Sunday is guesswork.

Mark it done the moment you do it, or at a fixed time each evening.
